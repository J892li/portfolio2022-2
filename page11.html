Background

The falling angle is a first-person role-playing/puzzle adventure PC game. Weixiang and I coded this game using JAVA on the game engine Godot. We created this game on an interactive design course during my study abroad at Nan-yang Technological University, Singapore.

The falling angle uses a first-person view, and the player will be 'Wendy,' a student at NTU. Your friend Claire was and found dead. Surprisingly, you remember nothing during the night she passed away, and you might be the killer. The player needs to view all the scenes, collect evidence, and finally choose the real murderer to end the game.

Corresponding to the topic of "self-portrait," we are trying to discuss how people portrait themselves in reality. Perhaps the reason we feel the need to take another, and yet another selfie, is because we never fully correspond to what we want to see in ourselves.

 I was responsible for prototyping, and I implemented UI into the game.
Peers and profs commented on our game as emotional and full of innovation 

NTU Map - Jin

How to play the game ？

 We designed the game so that the player needs to travel around and find her lost memories. The map contains seven trigger points to activate lost memories. The dialogue boxes will guide the player in the game.

We created a graphical game map based on a real campus map. After completing the tour, the player will decide who is the actual killer. 

Challenges


#1 How to communicate with our players?
Our game has no narrations, only background music, and audio effects. The question is how to communicate with the player and guide the player through the game?
Solution: I used dialogue boxes to solve the issue. Dialogue boxes can convey speech in the game, mimicking how people speak in the real world. The advantages of dialogue boxes are simple. It supported colors, symbols, and custom layouts. I designed a half-transparent dialogue box, and it helped to advanced the game virtually.
For other visual elements and transitions, we shoot real-world scenes on Campus. Jin and RR were responsible for that part.

#2 Code the game, okay, I learned it from scratch
Our prof recommended a game engine named Godot, but I never used a game engine before.
Solution:
Fortunately, I am experienced with JavaScript, and I coded a bit Java before , so I have a solid foundation before I start.
I coded six scenes independently, include transitions, and embed media. I also consulted our professor and YouTube channel a lot to learn how to code the game. I also collaborated with another coder Weixiang; he helped me with many bugs and logic issues.
Finally, using videos, pictures, and my UI design, we developed our game successfully.
