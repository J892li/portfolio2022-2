Background

Grab is the largest mobile technology company located in Southeast Asia. However, Singapore regulates e-scooter strictly due to safety concerns. Our goal was to revamping the scooting journey within the NTU campus to ensure a smoother utilization experience of the e-scooters while ensuring a stricter law regulation.

In this project, my roles and responsibilities were to build draft prototypes and facilitate design direction for a safe scooter journey.  

User Pain Points & Issues

  1. Transportation Law : Singapore has restrictions on the riding of e-scooters on sidewalks because users might put the scooter on public paths, and it might cause a safety issue for pedestrians 
  2. Users concerns: While experimenting the e-scooter services within universities, students are worried if they got injured and they cannot reach hep immediately
  3. Emerging Market. Grab has concerns with the potential market and profit margin of these services 

Challenges 


#1 How to prevent public mess
Solution : Automatic docking System
We created a docking station to collect and auto charge the scooter to reduces mess on the public path.
It also has an auto-locking/unlock system, so users can use the App to book a scooter before they arrive.
The docking system was built for stricter regulation in the future. It also enables our users to have a convenient and utterly automatic scooter service journey. 



#2 What if users are injured , and no one noticed?
Solution : Grab Aid
We added a built-in gyro sensor measuring the angle of tilt of the e-scooter. Once reached the angle, it will trigger an automatic safety feature connected to emergency aid enabling medical attention to be given to scooter usersâ€™ in time of unprecedented and heavy injuries.


#3 How can we expand the potential market?
Solution : Google plugin
We  Integrated the Grab Wheels plugin into Google maps alongside other currently available Grab services. It will provide easy access to the use of scooter services.

Summary

Reflection

Successes:
1. The idea of an automatic docking system can cope with future restrictions
2. The Grab Aid feature is a human-centered design and enhanced the services of the experience successfully

Things I Would Do Differently Next Time:
1. I will try to focus on how to deliver services more than focusing on laws and regulations if it is possible
